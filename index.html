Boards of Canada


Boards of Canada is a scottish electronic music duo consisting of brothers Michael Sandison and Marcus Eoin. They are one of the most influential electronic artists of the last 20 years.

Here's a timeline of Boards of Canada's history:

  • 1986: Boards of canada is born
  • 1990: The band releases first studio release, the Twoism EP
  • 1996: Made a semi-private album release titled BOC Maxima. Hi Scores EP is released.
  • 1998: Boards of Canada releases debut studio album, Music Has the Right to Children
  • 2000: Releases four-track EP, In a Beautiful Place Out in the Country
  • 2002: Second studio album titled Geogaddi is released
  • 2005: The Campfire Headbase LP is released
  • 2013: Boards of Canada releases another studio album after eight years, named Tomorrow's Harvest
  • 2016: The band releases two remixes: the first on 12 January, of NEVERMEN'S "Mr Mistake"; the second on 22 February, remix of "Sisters" by Odd Nosdam
  • 2017: Releases instrumental version of "Mr Mistake" remix and a remix of "Sometimes" by The Sexual Objects
  • 2019: Boards of Canada is featured with a 2-hour mixtape in WXAXRXP, a celebration for Warp Records' 30th anniversary.
